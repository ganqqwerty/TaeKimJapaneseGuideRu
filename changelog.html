  • 2004/10/12 - For those of you in doubt, yes, I'm still working on this guide though I can only afford to spend a few minutes a day due to a shortage of free time. I have just started adding popup tool-tips that show the reading and meaning of every Japanese word in the examples. It seems like the perfect compromise between spoon feeding the reading of kanji every time (which will become a crutch and lets you get away with never learning kanji) and forcing even complete beginners to continuously look up every single word (which is frustrating). Now, if you know all the words, everything is the same as before. However, if you run into a word you don't know, you can just hold the mouse cursor over it for a few seconds and the reading and meaning will pop up. Of course, you really should look up each new kanji for its readings and stroke order but this will allow people even with a limited vocabulary to read through the lessons more smoothly. With Firefox, the word that is translated will also be highlighted upon the mouse over. It doesn't look like IE supports this, though. I have finished the basic grammar section and plan to do a lesson a day for the rest.
